In HTML, headings are defined using the h1, h2, h3, h4, h5, and h6 elements. These elements represent headings of different levels, with h1 being the highest (most important) level and h6 being the lowest.

Here's an explanation of each heading element:
h1: Represents the highest level of heading. It is typically used for main headings or titles on a page.
h2:Represents a level-2 heading. It is used for subheadings or sections that are slightly less important than the main heading.
h4 to h6 Represent heading levels of decreasing importance. They are used for subsections, sub-subsections, and so on.
Here's an example of how heading elements are used in HTML:
Heading Example

Main Heading

This is the main content of the page.

Subheading 1

This is a subsection under the main heading.

Subheading 2

Another subsection under the main heading.

Sub-subheading

A sub-subsection under one of the subheadings.

In this example, the h1 element is used for the main heading, h2 elements are used for subheadings, and h3 is used for a sub-subheading. The choice of heading level conveys the hierarchy and importance of the content on the page. Headings also play a crucial role in improving accessibility and search engine optimization (SEO).
